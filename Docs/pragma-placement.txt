Pragmas with File Scope

may be placed anywhere in the File,
before or after a token, another pragma or a comment,
but should ideally be placed before the module header.

Pragmas with Module Scope

may be placed at the end of the module header
before the terminating semicolon.

Pragmas with Type Declaration Scope

in record type declarations:
may be placed at the end of the record header,
before the field list sequence.

in other type declarations:
may be placed at the end of the declaration,
before the terminating semicolon.

Pragmas with Constant or Variable Definition Scope

may be placed at the end of the definition,
before the terminating semicolon;

Pragmas with Procedure Scope

may be placed at the end of the procedure header,
before the terminating semicolon.

Pragmas with Formal Parameter List Scope

may be placed at the end of the parameter list,
before the separating semicolon or closing parenthesis.

Pragmas with List Item Scope

may be placed after the parameter's identifier,
before the separating comma or following colon.

Pragmas with Point-Forward Scope

may be placed anywhere within the module body,
before or after a token, another pragma or a comment.

Pragmas without Scope

may be placed anywhere in the File,
before or after a token, another pragma or a comment,
but should ideally be placed after pragmas with file scope.
